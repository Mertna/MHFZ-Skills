※ Hunting Horn (HH) offers huge offensive and defensive buffs to the team, keep the monsters debuffed, while having a high damage output. However it is difficult to play, as it has near no mobility, evasions (rolling is your only option), and most attacks lock you in place and make you quite vulnerable. The Horn should always have Red and Purple notes, followed by either Yellow, Green or Blue note.
※ Buffing the team is not an excuse to use a passive gameplay. Any HH user should always stay very aggressive and near the monster, else they can end up being a dead weight for the team.
※ While HH built-in buffs are the best in the game, these buffs can get even higher with a buff support oriented build. Refer to Support page (Buff support) to look more into it.

-Recommended skills-
Thunder Clad×Thunder Clad10You gain a gauge below the character name and it is accumulated by evading, attacking, and moving.
When the gauge reaches its maximum you gain the following skills and benefits for 60 seconds.
All raw hitzones +5 (this stacks with other sources), Status Negate, Movement Speed Up+2, Weapon Handling, and Evade Distance Up.
If you take a hit that launches you before the bar was filled you will lose all progress.		Thunder clad is a skill with a ton of benefits, it always has high priority after all core skills are in the set.
HH lacks mobility and evasion, and Thunder Clad really helps it out. The gained movement speed stacks with Movement Up song effect, and gives HH a very good mobility when the skill activates, fixing one of the main issues of the weapon.
In the same way, Evasion Distance Up really helps with HH's only evasion options, rolling.
Thunder Clad is yet another reason that pushes you to use an aggressive playstyle with HH, as you should play the weapon.

Rush×Rush10Successful attacks and guard actions gradually increase an invisible meter that has two stages.
Stage one: Chest flashes and gains purple effect, giving +50 attack. Stage two: Flash repeats, gains +130 attack total, and running with weapons no longer consumes stamina.
The effect is completely reset when you use any items or sheathe your weapon.
The attack meter builds based on the Motion Value of the attacks used. As damage is not involved, this means you can build up Rush on a dead monster.
Melee weapons gain stages based purely on the total Motion Value dealt.
HH16 MV's800 MV's
※ With stage 2 active, the Extreme Style run has no stamina consumption but will not work if your stamina is literally 0.		Rush itself is another good reason to use Vampirism+2 on HH builds, as you do not want to sheathe to heal yourself.
Unlike Furious, getting hit does not reset Rush, so it does work pretty nice with HH's vulnerability. The Z skill is highly recommended.

Furious×Furious10Increase attack, affinity, elemental and status across 3 stages as you perform attacks AND evasions or guards.
You have to do a mix of damage and guarding/evading, only damage will not work!
Taking hits from monsters will drop one stage.
Stage 1True Attack +70Elemental 1.05xStatus 1.05xAffinity +10%
Stage 2True Attack +100Elemental 1.10xStatus 1.10xAffinity +25%
Stage 3True Attack +180Elemental 1.20xStatus 1.20xAffinity +40%
While Furious is usually in the recommended skills, it makes it to end game here.
It does give incredible buffs activated by being aggressive and rolling attacks, which at first sight is exactly what HH looks for.
However, as said HH's moveset makes the hunter vulnerable to attacks, and it is very likely that you will have to tank hits, which does drop Furious by one stage. Activating Furious is great, but keeping it active on HH is a very tough task, and does require you to know both the monster and your own timings precisely with no room for mistakes.
Despite Furious being very strong on HH, only slot it in if you know what you are doing.
